## Decisions

| Date | Decision | Reason | Alternatives Considered |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 2026-07-24 | One `negotiate.py` invocation is ONE round; the pilot orchestrates the loop | The revision between rounds is the pilot's judgment (rewrite the plan folding in accepted push-back) — that cannot be pure Python. So Python does one round + a `loop` continue-signal, and the pilot audits + revises + re-invokes, exactly like `codex-review` has Claude audit findings. Keeps Codex as input-to-judgment, never an oracle | A fully-automated Python loop that revises the plan itself (rejected: the revision IS the judgment; automating it removes the pilot); a single one-shot critique with no loop (rejected: the Owner explicitly wanted a real back-and-forth) |
| 2026-07-24 | The loop is hard-bounded by a round cap (default 2) | The north star is spending scarce flagship tokens well; two brains arguing without a cap is the failure mode. `loop_should_continue` always stops at the cap regardless of remaining concerns, and the final-round prompt tells Codex to resolve to a decision. Cap 2 matches the design conversation: "one round of push-back, then the pilot decides" | No cap, converge naturally (rejected: unbounded flagship burn); cap 1 / one-shot (rejected: no room for Codex to see the revision); a token budget instead of rounds (rejected: rounds are the legible unit for a negotiation) |
| 2026-07-24 | A `converged: true` that still lists blocking concerns is NOT trusted | The flag and the list can contradict; trusting the flag would paper over a real concern. Fail-safe toward resolving it — keep negotiating (until the cap), never treat a contradiction as agreement. Same "don't report the reassuring case from an absence of evidence" discipline that ran through the 0.11–0.12 work | Trust the flag (rejected: papers over a stated blocker); error out (rejected: a contradiction is the model's, not a reason to abort the pilot's flow — degrade to 'keep talking') |
| 2026-07-24 | Codex critiques the plan as an EQUAL peer, and the critique includes a decomposition with owner + tier | The synergy engine's next step (delegate/execute) needs a task map with per-task owner (claude/codex/either) and model tier (flagship/workhorse/cheap). Having Codex propose it during the critique means the two brains agree on the split as part of converging — and the live dogfood put every task of a small feature on workhorse/cheap, exactly the token-thrift the design targets | Critique only, decompose separately (rejected: the split is part of what they should agree on); pilot decomposes alone (rejected: loses Codex's view on where flagship earns its cost) |
| 2026-07-24 | Every schema property is listed in `required` (strict-mode fix) | The live dogfood — not the fake-Codex, which ignores schemas — surfaced a 400: OpenAI structured output rejects a strict (`additionalProperties:false`) schema unless every property in an object is also `required`. The `decomposition` items had `why` optional. Making it required also forces Codex to justify each task's owner/tier, a bonus | Loosen `additionalProperties` (rejected: strict schemas are why the output is reliable); mark `why` optional in a non-strict schema (rejected: the reliability of the locked shape is worth a required justification) |
